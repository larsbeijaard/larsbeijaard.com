Feed The Beast

The Project Three developers and 2 artists were asked to create a local multiplayer game.
So we did. We created a 2D local multiplayer FFA dungeon crawler game. The goal of the game is that you need to collect food that is spread around the map and put it in your bowl. The person who has the least amount of food will get toasted (eliminated) by the dragon. You can also place traps, each trap has their own affect and will slow the trapped player down in their progress.
What have I learnt? I have learnt that discussing and setting code standards is very important. Because the codebase we currently have is a complete mess. Because everyone uses a different code style, it makes the codebase inconsistent and cluttered. (I made a snippet for the example. Take a look at the 3 variables. One of them is using a 'm_' the others do not. the '[SerializedField]' is not up top, etc.
I have also learnt that good communication is necessary, this prevents that multiple people are working on the same thing, or that two systems made by two different people does not match at all when they should.

Save and Load

The Project I was using the save and load functionality quite often in my projects. But I could not implement one in the other, so I had to write a save and load system over and over again just to fit that project.
That is why I made this, this save and load system could be imported into every project that needs to have some data saved and loaded.
What have I learnt? I have learned a bit on how to use T variables , because you can not use a struct as parameter. I have also learnt that you need to avoid T variables as much as you can, because they can cause confusion in your projects. They can, because they can be every kind of variable (class, float, third party variables, etc), which could make them hard to read without good commenting.

HomeworkNotifyer

The Project The amount of assignments you get sometimes could be a bit overwhelming, so for that reason I created a Discord Bot. The bot has a simple concept. You assign the bot homework with the description of what you need to do exactly (and add reference links, etc.) and the deadline.
When the deadline is coming closer and closer, the bot will notify you that the deadline is coming closer and closer.
What have I learnt? I have learnt working inside the C# Discord API. I have also learnt how to setup, run and maintain a Discord bot.
Currently, the bot is being held up on my Raspberry PI.

House Party

The Project At home some of our lamps are Philips Hue lamps. And you can control them by using an app on your phone (Android: download, iOS: download).
So I though: 'There must be some sort or API for controlling those lamps'. And I was right. You can send simple HTTP request to the Bridge (Philips Hue light station) and sent some data with it. For example, the brightness of the light.
What have I learnt? I have learnt that those lights are way more fun to play with then they should. I have never payed so much attention to light bulbs and see how their brightness is getting changed rapidly.
